general gravitational waves

A more general, over-arching look at gravitational waves. This is more of a popular science look at this to simplify the ideas for the contest. Contains multiple sources. They can be found at the end of the notes.


What are gravitational waves?

  • Gravitational waves are ripples in the fabric of space time
  • They create distortions in the curvature of the fabric that which the world around us interacts with
  • They are produced and created by accelerations in mass of any scale
  • However, only the super-massive objects can barely be detected

Example

  • Consider a trampoline that has a rock and a leaf on it
  • The mass of the rock causes the trampoline to bend around it while the leaf does not disturb it
  • The trampoline can be thought of space-time as for massive objects, like the sun, can cause the bending of its fabric
  • Gravitational waves happen when two massive objects interact with one another, causing space-time to observe waves from it

How are gravitational waves detected?

  • To detect gravitational waves, one would need a constant in nature to measure expansion or contractions
  • If a system to measure this phenomena expands and contracts along with the target, then it is flawed
  • For this to work the universial constant of the speed of light needs to be considered
  • If a target length expands, light will take a larger time to reach that distance allowing for this measurement
  • The LIGO experiment takes advantage of this by extending two perpendicular arms with lasers that can be used to measure this change in physical distance
