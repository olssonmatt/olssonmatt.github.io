  • not a complete list and continually adding to it!

  • Switzerland
    • CERN - Large Hadron Collider: CERN is the European laboratory for particle physics and one of the world's leading scientific research laboratories. CERN's business is pure research - physicists and engineers use the world's largest and most complex scientific instruments to study Nature's tiniest building blocks, the fundamental particles, to find out how our world and the Universe work.
    • Einstein House: The Einsteinhaus is a museum and a former residence of Albert Einstein. It is located on Kramgasse No. 49 in Bern, Switzerland.
    • Zytglogge Bern: The Zytglogge is a landmark medieval tower in Bern, Switzerland. The tower inspired a young patent clerk named Albert Einstein, and changed the way we think about the universe.
  • Italy
    • Museum of Galileo: Museo Galileo is located in Florence, Italy, in Piazza dei Giudici, along the River Arno and close to the Uffizi Gallery. The museum, dedicated to astronomer and scientist Galileo Galilei, is housed in Palazzo Castellani, an 11th-century building which was then known as the Castello d'Altafronte.
    • Archimede & Leonardo da Vinci Museum: Museum presenting interactive models of scientific inventions by Leonardo da Vinci & Archimedes.
  • England
    • Westminster Abbey - Sir Isaac Newton's Tomb: Westminster Abbey is an Anglican church in London, England. It has been the location of the coronations of English monarchs, and Sir Isaac Newton is buried there.
    • Woolsthorpe Manor House: Woolsthorpe Manor is the birthplace and family home of Sir Isaac Newton, located in Lincolnshire, England.
    • Bletchley Park: Bletchley Park is an English country house and estate in Milton Keynes that became the principal centre of Allied code-breaking during the Second World War.
  • United States
    • Space Centre Houston: Space Centre Houston is a science museum that serves as the official visitor center of NASA Johnson Space Center in Houston.
    • Kennedy Space Centre: The Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex is the visitor center at NASA's Kennedy Space Center on Merritt Island, Florida. It features exhibits, historic spacecraft, IMAX theaters, and tours.
  • Sweden
    • Nobel Prize Museum: The Nobel Prize Museum is located in Stockholm, Sweden, showcasing the history of the Nobel Prize and its laureates.
  • Canada
    • SNOLABS: SNOLAB is a world-class science facility located deep underground in a nickel mine near Sudbury, Ontario, Canada.
    • TRIUMF: TRIUMF is Canada's particle accelerator centre, home to the world's largest cyclotron of its kind.
    • Fathom Five National Marine Park: A National Marine Conservation Area in Lake Huron, Ontario, Canada, ideal for stargazing, exploring shipwrecks, and enjoying freshwater ecosystems.
  • Czechia
    • Kepler Museum: The Kepler Museum in Prague, Czech Republic, is dedicated to the German astronomer Johannes Kepler.
  • Serbia
    • Nikola Tesla Museum: The Nikola Tesla Museum in Belgrade, Serbia, honors and displays the life and work of Nikola Tesla.
